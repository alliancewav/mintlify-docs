Use this page when something is broken, suspicious, abusive, or legally concerning. The fastest path depends on the type of issue you are reporting.

---

## Choose the Right Reporting Path

| Issue type | Best path |
|------------|-----------|
| Comment abuse or spam | Use **More** -> **Report comment** on the comment itself |
| Playback, upload, billing, or account bug | Use the public contact form or email BeatPass |
| Copyright, ownership, or takedown concern | Use the [Copyright & DMCA](/help/legal/copyright-and-dmca) guidance and contact BeatPass directly |
| Security concern about your account | Secure your account first, then contact BeatPass |

---

## Reporting In-App Abuse

BeatPass currently has a verified built-in reporting action for comments.

### Report a Comment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the comment menu">
    Click **More** on the comment you want to report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the report action">
    Select **Report comment**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let BeatPass review it">
    BeatPass confirms the report and queues it for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You only need to report a comment once. If the issue is broader than a single comment, contact BeatPass directly with links and context.
</Note>

## Reporting Bugs and Broken Behavior

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the contact form">
    Open the [Contact Support](/help/contact-support) page, or email [contact@beatpass.ca](mailto:contact@beatpass.ca).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the area that is broken">
    Start your message with a short label like "Playback bug," "Upload issue," or "Billing problem."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe what happened">
    Include the page involved, what you expected, what happened instead, and any error text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add proof when useful">
    Attach screenshots or recordings if they make the problem easier to understand.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What Makes a Good Bug Report

Include:

- The exact BeatPass page or URL
- The track, playlist, producer profile, request, or invoice involved
- Your device and browser
- The exact error message, if one appeared
- Whether the problem happens every time or only once

## Reporting Copyright or Policy Concerns

Use direct support contact for:

- Suspected stolen uploads
- Ownership disputes
- Unauthorized use concerns
- Questions about a takedown or rights issue

When the issue is legal or rights-related, include:

- The exact URL
- The track or producer name
- Why you believe the content is unauthorized or non-compliant
- Any proof you can provide

<Warning>
  For formal takedown requests, use the requirements in [Copyright & DMCA](/help/legal/copyright-and-dmca). That page explains what BeatPass needs for a proper copyright notice.
</Warning>

---

## Reporting Security or Account Risk

If you think someone accessed your account or changed something without your permission:

1. Change your password right away
2. Review [Active Sessions](/help/account-settings/active-sessions)
3. Disconnect anything that should not still have access
4. Contact BeatPass with the affected account email and a short timeline of what happened

---

## Before You Send the Report

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Playback or loading issue">
    Try a refresh, sign out and back in, or test another browser before you report the issue. If it still happens, mention what you already tried.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Upload failure">
    Confirm the file matches the current BeatPass upload rules first, especially format and metadata requirements. Then report the exact point where the upload stopped.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Billing or download issue">
    Gather the invoice date, track name, plan name, or purchase page link before you contact BeatPass. Those details usually matter more than a general description.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
