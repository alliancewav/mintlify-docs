## Overview

BeatPass gives you control over your personal information and how it's shared. This guide explains your privacy options and how to configure them.

---

## Profile Visibility

### Public vs Private Profiles

| Setting | Who Can See |
|---------|-------------|
| **Public** | Anyone on the internet |
| **Followers Only** | Only users who follow you |
| **Private** | Only you (not available for producers) |

<Note>
  Producers must maintain public profiles for their tracks to be discoverable.
</Note>

### Configuring Profile Visibility

1. Go to **Settings > Privacy**
2. Under **Profile Visibility**, select your preference
3. Save changes

## Information You Control

### Profile Information

| Field | Visibility Options |
|-------|-------------------|
| Display Name | Always visible (required) |
| Bio | Follows profile visibility |
| Profile Photo | Follows profile visibility |

Control who sees your activity:

| Activity | Options |
|----------|---------|
| Listening History | Private, followers, or public |
| Saved Tracks | Private or public |

| Following List | Private or public |

## Producer Privacy

### Earnings Information

Your earnings data is always private:

- Total earnings visible only to you
- Per-track contribution shown only in your dashboard
- No earnings data is shared publicly

### Analytics Sharing

| Data Type | Shared Publicly? |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Play counts | Yes (on tracks) |
| Detailed analytics | No (your dashboard only) |
| Listener demographics | No |
| Earnings/contribution | No |

## Promotional Email Tracking

BeatPass uses standard email analytics on promotional messages so we can understand whether our communications are helpful. These controls apply only to marketing and promotional emailsâ€”not transactional messages like receipts or security alerts.

### What Is Tracked

- **1x1 pixel**: Promotional emails include a tiny remote image that records when the email is opened. The pixel request logs your BeatPass account ID (if applicable), the timestamp, IP address, approximate location (derived from IP), and email client metadata provided by your mail app (e.g., user agent).
- **Tracked links**: Promotional links route through a BeatPass redirect that records the same details as above along with the destination URL and whether the click came from an email.

### How to Opt Out

- Go to **Settings > Notifications > Marketing Communications** and disable marketing and promotional emails.
- Alternatively, use the **unsubscribe** link included at the bottom of each promotional email.

### Reduce Tracking in Your Email Client

- **Disable remote image loading** to prevent the 1x1 pixel from loading. Most email apps provide a "Block external images" or "Protect from tracking" option.
- **Avoid clicking redirected links** or copy the visible URL into your browser to skip link tracking when possible.
- Use a **privacy-focused email client** that blocks tracking pixels and rewrites tracking links automatically.

## Data You Can Access

### Viewing Your Data

You can view all data BeatPass stores about you:

1. Go to **Settings > Privacy > Your Data**
2. Click **View My Data**
3. Review stored information

### Downloading Your Data

Request a copy of your data:

1. Go to **Settings > Privacy > Your Data**
2. Click **Request Data Export**
3. Receive download link via email (may take 24-48 hours)

Export includes:
- Profile information
- Uploaded tracks and metadata
- Analytics history
- Messages (your messages only)
- Transaction history

## Data Deletion

### Deleting Specific Data

| Data Type | How to Delete |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tracks | Backstage > Tracks > Delete |
| Messages | Conversation settings > Delete |
| Playlists | Library > Playlist > Delete |
| Saved tracks | Remove from library |

### Account Deletion

To delete your entire account:

1. Go to **Settings > Account > Delete Account**
2. Confirm your decision
3. Enter password to verify

<Warning>
  Account deletion is permanent. All data, tracks, earnings history, and content will be removed and cannot be recovered.
</Warning>

### What Happens After Deletion

| Data | Handling |
|------|----------|
| Profile | Immediately removed |
| Tracks | Removed from catalog |
| Earnings history | Retained for tax/legal compliance (anonymized) |
| Messages | Removed from conversations |
| Analytics | Aggregated data retained (anonymized) |

## Third-Party Access

### Connected Applications

View and manage third-party apps connected to your account:

1. Go to **Settings > Privacy > Connected Apps**
2. Review all connected applications
3. Revoke access for any you don't recognize

### What Apps Can Access

Authorized apps may access:
- Public profile information
- Scopes you explicitly granted
- Never: passwords, payment info, private messages
