When you download a beat from BeatPass, you receive a license that grants you specific rights to use that beat. This section explains the different license types and what they allow.

## License Types

BeatPass offers two types of licenses:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Non-Exclusive License" href="/help/legal/license-terms/non-exclusive" icon="users">
    Included with all BeatPass subscriptions. Multiple artists can license the same beat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Exclusive License" href="/help/legal/license-terms/exclusive" icon="crown">
    Purchase full exclusive rights to a beat. Only you can use it commercially.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Non-Exclusive | Exclusive |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Who can use the beat** | Multiple artists | Only you |
| **Price** | Included in subscription | Separate purchase |
| **License duration** | Perpetual | Perpetual |
| **Files included** | WAV, MP3 | WAV, WAV Tagged, MP3, Stems |
| **Projects allowed** | 1 per download | Unlimited |
| **Producer credit required** | Yes | Varies by agreement |
| **Content ID allowed** | No | Yes |
| **Live performance limit** | 50 attendees | Unlimited |
| **TV/Film/Commercials** | No | Yes |

## What All Licenses Include

Regardless of license type, you always get:

- **Perpetual rights** — Your license never expires
- **Worldwide territory** — Use the beat anywhere in the world
- **Commercial use** — Make money from songs using the beat
- **Your ownership** — You own your final song (the beat itself remains owned by the producer)

## What You Cannot Do With Any License

- **Claim you made the beat** — The producer remains the creator
- **Resell the beat itself** — You can't sell the beat file to others
- **Transfer your license** — Licenses are non-transferable
