## Overview

When you license a beat on BeatPass, you receive specific rights to use it in your projects. This guide explains each license type in plain language.

<Note>
  For how these legal license types map to Backstage track settings (Standard vs Standard + Exclusive vs Exclusive Only), see [Licensing Models & Exclusive Availability](/product-guides/licensing-models-exclusive).
</Note>

---

## License Types

### Non-Exclusive License

<Card title="Non-Exclusive" icon="copy">
  You get usage rights, but the beat remains available for others to license.
</Card>

**What You Get:**
- Right to use the beat in your recordings
- Commercial release rights with **unlimited** streaming and distribution under standard BeatPass non-exclusive terms
- MP3 and/or WAV file downloads

**What You Don't Get:**
- Exclusive ownership
- Right to prevent others from licensing the same beat
- Right to resell the beat

**Best For:**
- Testing beats before committing
- Limited releases (singles, mixtapes)
- Budget-conscious artists
- Multiple beat purchases

---

### Exclusive License

<Card title="Exclusive" icon="lock">
  You become the sole licensee. The beat is removed from public sale.
</Card>

**What You Get:**
- Full ownership rights to the recording
- Beat removed from marketplace (no new licenses issued)
- Unlimited streams/sales
- High-quality WAV file download

**What You Don't Get:**
- Copyright to the composition (unless negotiated)
- Right to claim you wrote the music
- Prior non-exclusive licenses revoked (they remain valid for their terms)

**Best For:**
- Serious commercial releases
- Artists wanting unique sound
- Major label submissions
- Long-term projects

## License Comparison

| Feature | Non-Exclusive | Exclusive |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Price | Lower | Higher |
| Beat availability | Remains for sale | Removed from sale |
| Stream limits | **No**  unlimited audio and video streams |
| Sales limits | **No**  unlimited digital distribution and copies |
| File format | WAV download | WAV download |
| Other licensees | Possible | No new ones |

<Note>
  All licenses include a high-quality WAV file. For stems, contact the producer directly to arrange separate delivery.
</Note>

## Common Terms Explained

### Distribution Limits

Under the current BeatPass non-exclusive subscription license configuration:

- Audio streams and digital distributions are **unlimited** for the licensed release.
- The license is **worldwide** and **perpetual** (it does not expire for that project).
- Some uses still have specific limits (for example, live performances over a certain audience size or certain sync/film/TV uses may require an upgrade).

<Note>
  Always refer to your actual license certificate for the exact terms in effect for your download, including any performance or usage caps.
</Note>

### Commercial Use

Both license types allow commercial use, meaning you can:
- Sell your songs
- Earn streaming revenue
- Use in monetized videos
- Perform publicly

### Credit Requirements

Most licenses require credit to the producer:
- In track metadata
- In liner notes or credits
- Format: "Produced by [Producer Name]"

Check your specific license certificate for exact requirements.

## Rights Breakdown

### What Producers Keep

Even with exclusive licenses, producers typically retain:

| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Composition copyright** | Unless specifically transferred |
| **Production credit** | Name credit in all uses |
| **Publishing share** | Portion of publishing royalties (negotiable) |
| **Portfolio rights** | Right to showcase in their catalog |

### What Artists Get

| Right | Non-Exclusive | Exclusive |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| Recording rights | Yes | Yes |
| Commercial release | Yes (limited) | Yes (unlimited) |
| Sync licensing | Sometimes | Usually |
| Derivative works | Limited | Yes |
| Sublicensing | No | Sometimes |

## Usage Scenarios

### Can I Use This Beat For...?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Streaming Platforms (Spotify, Apple Music)">
    **Yes** - Both license types allow streaming platform distribution within specified limits.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="YouTube Videos">
    **Yes** - Monetized videos are typically allowed. Check sync rights for non-exclusive.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="TikTok/Instagram">
    **Yes** - Social media use is generally permitted under standard licenses.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Physical Sales (CDs, Vinyl)">
    **Usually** - Check your specific license. Often included in premium tiers.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="TV/Film Placement">
    **Depends** - Sync rights must be included. More common with exclusive licenses.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Remixing or Sampling">
    **Exclusive only** - Non-exclusive typically doesn't allow derivative works.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Reselling the Beat">
    **Never** - Licenses don't include resale rights.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## License Certificates

### What's Included

Every purchase generates a license certificate containing:

- Unique license ID
- Licensee information
- Licensed track details
- License type and terms
- Usage rights summary
- Effective date
- Audio fingerprint reference

### Storing Your License

<Warning>
  Keep your license certificate safe. It's your proof of rights.
</Warning>

- Download immediately after purchase
- Store in multiple locations (cloud + local)
- Keep indefinitely
- Available anytime in Library > Licenses

### Verification

Licenses can be verified at any time:
- Use the license verification page
- Enter your license ID
- Confirm authenticity and terms

## Upgrading Licenses

### From Non-Exclusive to Exclusive

If available (beat not exclusively sold):

1. Contact the producer
2. Negotiate upgrade pricing
3. Complete new purchase
4. Receive updated license certificate

<Note>
  Not all beats allow upgrades. Some producers don't offer exclusive options.
</Note>

### When to Upgrade

Consider upgrading when:
- Your release exceeds license limits
- You want to prevent future licensees
- You're pursuing major label interest
- The beat is central to your brand
