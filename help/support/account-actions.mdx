## Overview

BeatPass may take action on accounts that violate community guidelines or terms of service. This guide explains what actions mean and how to appeal if you believe a decision was made in error.

---

## Types of Account Actions

### Warning

| What It Means | Impact |
|---------------|--------|
| First-time or minor violation | No immediate restriction |
| Notification sent | Serves as formal notice |
| Recorded on account | Multiple warnings lead to escalation |

**What to do:** Read the warning, understand the violation, avoid repeating.

### Content Removal

| What It Means | Impact |
|---------------|--------|
| Specific content violated guidelines | That content is removed |
| May include warning | Depends on severity |
| Other content unaffected | Unless related |

**What to do:** Review why content was removed; re-upload only if it can comply with guidelines.

### Temporary Restriction

| What It Means | Impact |
|---------------|--------|
| Feature access limited | Specific features disabled |
| Time-limited | Usually 7-30 days |
| Can still access account | With limitations |

**Examples:**
- Upload restriction
- Messaging restriction
- Commenting restriction

### Account Suspension

| What It Means | Impact |
|---------------|--------|
| Account access disabled | Cannot log in |
| Content hidden | Your content not visible |
| Time-limited or permanent | Depends on severity |

**Types:**
- **Temporary suspension** — 7-90 days depending on violation
- **Permanent suspension** — Account terminated

## Common Reasons for Action

### Content Violations

| Violation | Typical Action |
|-----------|---------------|
| Copyright infringement | Content removal + warning (1st), suspension (repeat) |
| Spam/misleading content | Content removal + warning |
| Prohibited content | Immediate removal, possible suspension |
| Metadata manipulation | Warning, content correction |

### Behavior Violations

| Violation | Typical Action |
|-----------|---------------|
| Harassment | Warning → restriction → suspension |
| Spam messaging | Messaging restriction |
| Fake engagement | Earnings forfeit, suspension |
| Terms of service violation | Varies by severity |

### Payment/Financial

| Violation | Typical Action |
|-----------|---------------|
| Fraudulent purchases | Account suspension |
| Chargeback abuse | Account suspension |
| Payout fraud | Permanent ban, legal action |

## Strike System

### How Strikes Work

| Strike Count | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| 1st strike | Warning + content removal |
| 2nd strike | Temporary restriction (7-30 days) |
| 3rd strike | Account suspension |

### Strike Expiration

- Strikes typically expire after **12 months** of good standing
- Severe violations may result in permanent strikes
- Multiple violations in short period escalate faster

## Appeal Process

### Who Can Appeal

- Any user who received account action
- Must appeal within **14 days** of action

### How to Appeal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the Action Notice">
    Understand exactly what action was taken and why.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Gather Evidence">
    Collect any evidence supporting your appeal:
    - Original content/context
    - Proof of ownership
    - Relevant communications
    - Explanation of circumstances
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Appeal">
    Email **appeals@beatpass.ca** with:
    - Subject: "Account Appeal - [Your Username]"
    - Description of the action taken
    - Why you believe it was in error
    - Supporting evidence
    - Your account email
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for Review">
    Appeals are reviewed within **5-10 business days**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Receive Decision">
    You'll receive an email with:
    - Appeal decision (upheld, overturned, modified)
    - Reasoning
    - Next steps if applicable
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What Makes a Strong Appeal

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be factual and specific | Be emotional or abusive |
| Provide evidence | Make unsupported claims |
| Acknowledge if you made a mistake | Deny everything without evidence |
| Explain context | Blame others without basis |
| Ask specific questions | Send multiple appeals |

---

## Appeal Timelines

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Submit appeal | Within 14 days of action |
| Acknowledgment | 1-2 business days |
| Review period | 5-10 business days |
| Decision delivery | Same day as decision |
| Second appeal (if available) | Within 7 days of first decision |

### Expedited Review

For urgent cases (e.g., wrongful suspension affecting income), note "URGENT" in your subject line with explanation.

---

## After Appeal Decision

### If Upheld (Action Stands)

| Situation | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| You disagree | May request second review (limited) |
| You accept | Wait out restriction period |
| Permanent action | Account cannot be recovered |

### If Overturned

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Account restored | Within 24 hours |
| Content reinstated | Within 24-48 hours |
| Strike removed | Immediately |
| Notification sent | Confirmation email |

### If Modified

Sometimes actions are modified (e.g., permanent reduced to temporary):

- New terms communicated
- May require acknowledgment
- Clock restarts on modified action

## Preventing Account Actions

### Best Practices

- Read and follow [Community Guidelines](/help/legal/content-guidelines)
- Respond to warnings promptly
- Don't repeat violations
- Keep communications professional
- When unsure, ask before acting

### Red Flags to Avoid

| Activity | Risk |
|----------|------|
| Mass uploading duplicate content | Spam detection |
| Automated engagement | Fraud detection |
| Selling off-platform | Terms violation |
| Sharing account credentials | Security violation |
| Harassment or threats | Immediate suspension |

---

## Special Cases

### Copyright/DMCA Actions

DMCA takedowns have a separate process:

- See [Copyright & DMCA](/help/legal/copyright) for counter-notification process
- Different timelines apply
- May involve legal obligations

### Payment Disputes

For payment-related suspensions:

- Contact support directly
- Provide payment documentation
- Resolution may require bank/card issuer cooperation

### Mistaken Identity

If you believe your account was actioned due to confusion with another user:

- Provide identifying information
- Explain the confusion
- Include evidence of your identity

---

## Regaining Trust

After a restriction or suspension ends:

| Recommendation | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| Start slow | Don't immediately push limits |
| Review guidelines | Ensure you understand rules |
| Monitor communications | Watch for any notices |
| Maintain good standing | 12 months clears most history |
