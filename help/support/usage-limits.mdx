## Overview

BeatPass implements usage limits to ensure fair access, prevent abuse, and maintain platform performance. This page consolidates all per-feature limits in one reference.

---

## Upload Limits

### Track Uploads

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Formats" icon="file-audio">
    **WAV, FLAC** (lossless formats recommended for best quality)
  </Card>
  <Card title="Practical Guidelines" icon="clock">
    Most uploads work best when files are reasonably sized (for example, under ~100MB and under ~15 minutes) to keep processing fast. These are **guidelines, not hard platform limits**—very large files may be constrained by browser, network, or infrastructure limits.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Upload Volume" icon="calendar">
    Approved producers are not subject to a fixed daily upload cap. Upload activity is monitored for abuse, but there is no enforced per-day or per-track quota.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  *Excessive uploads in a short period may trigger rate limiting. BeatPass only accepts lossless formats — no MP3 or compressed audio.
</Note>

## What Happens at Limits

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Rate Limit Hit" icon="gauge-high">
    You'll receive a **429 error**. Wait for the cooldown period, then retry your request.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Storage Limit Hit" icon="database">
    Cannot save more items. Remove old items first to free up space.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Daily Limit Hit" icon="calendar-xmark">
    Wait until the next day (UTC midnight) for limits to reset.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Account Limit Hit" icon="user-xmark">
    Feature disabled until the issue is resolved. Contact support if needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Rate Limit Headers

API responses include rate limit information:

```http
X-RateLimit-Limit: 60
X-RateLimit-Remaining: 45
X-RateLimit-Reset: 1700000000
```
