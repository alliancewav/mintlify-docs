## Quick Answers

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is BeatPass free to use?">
    Yes. You can browse and listen with a free account. Licensed downloads require a paid subscription.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I keep my licenses if I cancel?">
    Yes. Licenses obtained while subscribed remain valid after cancellation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do producers earn money?">
    Producers earn through subscription-based contribution payouts and through direct sales such as exclusive purchases. See [Earnings](/help/earnings/index).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What files do I get when I download?">
    The exact files depend on the track and license flow. BeatPass's standard subscription downloads should not be treated as a guaranteed fixed bundle for every track.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use beats on Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube?">
    BeatPass licenses cover many release scenarios, but the exact permissions depend on the license type. Standard subscription licensing still has restrictions such as attribution requirements and no Content ID registration. See [Releasing Music](/help/downloads-and-licensing/releasing-music).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I become a producer on BeatPass?">
    Use the Producer Program flow. BeatPass currently supports becoming a producer, claiming an existing profile, or requesting verification. See [Producer Program](/help/producer-program/index).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the platform fee?">
    A `12% + $3.00 flat` fee is currently added on top of eligible BeatPass checkouts. For exclusive purchases, the producer keeps 100% of the listed price. See [Platform Fee](/help/billing/platform-fee).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I collaborate with other producers?">
    Yes. BeatPass supports credited collaborators on tracks. See [Collaborations](/help/uploading/collaborations).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
