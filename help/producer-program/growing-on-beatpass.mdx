Whether you are newly approved or already building a catalog, growth on BeatPass comes from consistent uploads, accurate metadata, and keeping your beats easy to discover and license.

---

## Upload Consistently

Consistency is one of the biggest growth factors on BeatPass.

- **Recent activity matters** — The [contribution system](/help/earnings/contribution-system/formula) applies time decay, so new activity helps your catalog stay competitive
- **Catalog depth helps** — More strong beats give subscribers and buyers more chances to find something that fits
- **Aim for a sustainable rhythm** — The producer request form currently asks you to commit to at least 2 beats per month

<Tip>
  Quality still matters more than raw volume. A small, well-maintained catalog usually outperforms a large catalog full of incomplete uploads.
</Tip>

---

## Complete Your Metadata

Metadata is one of the easiest growth levers because it improves searchability and makes your beats easier to license.

## Promote Your Music

BeatPass gives you the hosting and licensing tools, but outside promotion still matters.

### On Social Media

- Share direct links to your tracks and profile
- Post when you publish something new
- Give listeners context about the vibe, intended artist, or project
- Stay responsive when people engage with your releases

### Inside BeatPass

- Respond to beat requests that match your style
- Keep top-performing beats updated with better descriptions, tags, and artwork
- Offer exclusive options on beats that deserve premium positioning

---

## Review What Is Working

The producer workspace can change over time, but the growth questions stay the same:

- Which beats are getting the most plays?
- Which tracks keep earning over multiple payout cycles?
- Which uploads need better cover art or clearer tags?
- Which collections or collaborations are creating momentum?

Use those answers to decide what to upload next and which older tracks should be refreshed.

---

## Use Collaborations Intentionally

Collaborations expand your reach, but they also change how subscription revenue is attributed.

- Collaborative beats appear on every credited producer profile
- Contribution value is split equally across credited producers
- The uploader still manages the track, audio, and licensing settings

See [Collaborations](/help/uploading/collaborations) for the current collaboration rules.

---

## Organize Your Catalog

Use collections when they help the listener understand the release:

- Beat packs
- Themed series
- Seasonal drops
- Producer showcases

Collection tracks inherit artists, genres, and tags when uploaded, which can save time and keep a release consistent.

---

## Optimize Your Profile

Your producer profile is still your storefront:

- Write a clear bio
- Upload a professional photo and strong visuals
- Add professional credits where relevant
- Apply for verification if you are eligible

Verification adds a blue verified badge and unlocks features like Audio Recon and beat request alerts. See [Get Verified](/help/producer-program/application-types/get-verified) for the process.

---

## Growth Checklist

Use this as a recurring review:

- [ ] Uploading at least 2–4 tracks per month
- [ ] Cover art added to every track and collection
- [ ] Required fields completed on every upload
- [ ] Artist bio and profile photo up to date
- [ ] Older tracks reviewed for better tags, descriptions, and cover art
- [ ] Checking producer performance regularly
- [ ] Responding to beat requests that match your style
- [ ] Sharing new releases on social media
- [ ] Reviewing which tracks deserve exclusive pricing
