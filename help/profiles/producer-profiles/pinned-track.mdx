# Pinned Track

Pinning lets you feature one track at the top of your producer profile's **Discography** view.

## What a Pinned Track Does

When a track is pinned:

- It appears before the rest of the discography content
- It becomes the first track many visitors see
- It gives you manual control over your profile's first impression

## How to Pin a Track

Use the track context menu on your own track:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the track">
    Open one of your tracks from your producer profile or a track list where your own context menu is available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open track options">
    Right-click the track, or long-press on touch devices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the pin action">
    Select **Pin to profile**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Only one track can be pinned at a time. Pinning a new one replaces the old selection.
</Tip>

## How to Unpin It

Open the same context menu on the pinned track and choose **Unpin from profile**.

## What Makes a Good Pinned Track

Choose a track that:

- Represents your current sound well
- Has strong production and mix quality
- Supports the audience you want to attract
- Matches what you want new visitors to hear first

## When to Change It

Refresh your pinned track when:

- You release a new beat
- Your current brand direction changes
- You want to feature a track with stronger conversion or engagement

<Note>
The pinned track appears in your public producer profile. It is not managed as a separate library or dashboard setting.
</Note>
