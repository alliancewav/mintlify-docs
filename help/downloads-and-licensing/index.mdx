BeatPass lets you use tracks in two main ways:

- Download a track under a standard non-exclusive license when your account has download access
- Buy an exclusive license for eligible tracks with one-time Stripe checkout

This section explains what actually happens in the current product, including when licenses appear, where to find them, and what the platform verifies publicly.

<Info>
  **Important:** Track downloads and license certificate availability are related, but they are not the same step. A download can succeed even when the certificate is not ready to open yet.
</Info>

## License types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Non-exclusive license" icon="users">
    Standard BeatPass download license for eligible users. Multiple artists can license the same beat, and attribution is required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Exclusive license" icon="crown">
    One-time purchase for eligible tracks. After purchase, no new non-exclusive licenses are issued for that beat.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Fast comparison

| Topic | Non-exclusive | Exclusive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| How you get it | Download an eligible track | Buy **Buy Exclusive** on an eligible track |
| Other artists can still use the beat | Yes | No new licenses after purchase |
| Attribution required | Yes | Check the certificate terms |
| Content ID registration | No | Allowed under current default terms |
| Large live performances | No, capped at 50 attendees | Allowed under current default terms |
| Film/TV/broadcast/commercial/video games | Not allowed | Allowed under current default terms |
| Transferable | No | Not by default |

<Note>
  The exact exclusive terms come from the license preview and the certificate generated for that purchase. Do not assume every track uses the same custom terms forever.
</Note>

## How the current flow works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a track">
    On the track page, use **Download** for standard access or **Buy Exclusive** when the track is sold that way.
  </Step>
  <Step title="BeatPass checks access">
    Downloads require login, valid download access, and a current subscription state. Sold-exclusive tracks are blocked for everyone except the exclusive buyer and the track owner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your audio or purchase completes">
    Standard downloads deliver the track file. Exclusive purchases go through Stripe card checkout and generate an exclusive license on success.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your license">
    When BeatPass has an active license ready, you can open it from the post-download flow, the track page, or **Library** -> **Licenses**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify when needed">
    Every license can be checked on the public verification page using its UUID.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What is included today

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Standard track downloads" icon="download" defaultOpen>
    The current standard download flow downloads the track file itself. License access is handled separately through certificate views and **Library** -> **Licenses**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Exclusive purchases" icon="crown">
    On successful purchase, BeatPass generates the exclusive license, marks the beat as sold, attempts an immediate track download, and stores the license in **Library** -> **Licenses**. The final deliverables are defined by the license preview and certificate for that track.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Audio fingerprinting" icon="fingerprint">
    Certificate tools depend on completed BeatPass fingerprint data. That matters for certificate access and verification, but fingerprint completion is not a universal gate for every download.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
