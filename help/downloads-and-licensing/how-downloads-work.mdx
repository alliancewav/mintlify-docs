This page covers the current BeatPass download flow for standard track downloads. It explains who can download, what happens after you click **Download**, and when a license certificate becomes available.

<Info>
  Standard downloads require an eligible signed-in account. Exclusive checkout is covered separately in [Purchasing Exclusive](/help/downloads-and-licensing/exclusive-licenses/purchasing-exclusive).
</Info>

## Before you click Download

BeatPass checks several things before it lets a download start.

### Standard access rules

- You must be signed in
- Your account must have download access
- Your subscription cannot be overdue
- The track must still be available for non-exclusive download

### Important exceptions

- Track owners can download their own tracks
- Exclusive buyers can still download the track they purchased
- If a track has been sold exclusively, new non-exclusive downloads are blocked
- If a track is exclusive-only and you have not purchased it, standard download is not available

## The current download flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the download modal">
    Click **Download** on an eligible track. BeatPass opens the pre-download confirmation modal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the download">
    Use the **Download** action in the modal to start the file download to your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="BeatPass checks access again">
    The app confirms that your account is still allowed to download the track. If your subscription is overdue or the track is no longer eligible, the download is blocked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The track file downloads">
    BeatPass downloads the local track file. Producers downloading their own tracks do not need a generated license for that step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="License follow-up may appear">
    If you are a signed-in non-owner and the track fingerprint is completed, BeatPass can open a post-download license modal so you can view an existing certificate or use **Get License** when available.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens with licenses

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="If you already have an active license" icon="file-certificate" defaultOpen>
    The post-download flow can show **License Already Available** and send you to <a href="https://open.beatpass.ca/library/licenses">https://open.beatpass.ca/library/licenses</a>.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If you do not already have one" icon="plus">
    BeatPass attempts to reuse or generate the right license in the background. If the license is ready and the track fingerprint is completed, the **Get License** flow can open it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If fingerprinting is not complete yet" icon="fingerprint">
    The track can still download, but certificate access may not be ready. This is why downloads are not the same thing as certificate availability.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Where to find your licenses

Use **Library** -> **Licenses** in the app:

- App page: <a href="https://open.beatpass.ca/library/licenses">https://open.beatpass.ca/library/licenses</a>
- Search supports track name, artist name, licensee name, or license ID
- If you own tracks, the page can also show licenses granted to other users for your catalog

From that page you can:

- Open the printable certificate view
- Use **Verify License**
- Search across your license history

## Track availability rules

| Track state | What it means for standard downloads |
| --- | --- |
| **Non-exclusive only** | Standard download can be allowed if your account has access |
| **Both** | Standard download can still be allowed unless the track has been sold exclusively |
| **Exclusive only** | No standard download unless you are the owner or purchaser |
| **Sold** | No new standard downloads for other users |

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Download button is disabled or blocked" icon="triangle-exclamation" defaultOpen>
    Check that you are signed in, your account still has download access, your subscription is not overdue, and the track has not been sold exclusively.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The track downloaded but I do not see a certificate yet" icon="file-certificate">
    Check **Library** -> **Licenses** first. If the track fingerprint is still incomplete, the certificate view may not be ready yet even though the download succeeded.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I see an overdue subscription message" icon="credit-card">
    BeatPass blocks downloads for overdue subscriptions. Resolve the billing issue before trying again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I own the track but I do not see a license" icon="user">
    Owners can download their own tracks without generating a standard license certificate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
