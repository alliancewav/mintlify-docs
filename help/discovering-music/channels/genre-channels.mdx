Genre channels are specialized channels that showcase content within a specific musical style. They're one of the best ways to discover beats that match the sound you're looking for.

## What Are Genre Channels?

A genre channel is a dedicated page for a specific musical genre like Hip-Hop, R&B, Trap, or Pop. Each genre channel brings together:

- **Beats** in that genre
- **Albums** tagged with the genre
- **Producers** who create content in that style
- **Playlists** featuring that genre

## Finding Genre Channels

You can access genre channels in several ways:

### From the Homepage

1. Look for genre-related sections on the homepage
2. Click on any genre card or link
3. You'll be taken to that genre's dedicated channel

### From Search

1. Type the genre name in the search bar
2. Look for genre results in the search suggestions
3. Click to go to the genre channel

### From Beat Pages

1. View any beat's details
2. Find the genre tags listed on the beat
3. Click a genre to see its channel

## What's on a Genre Channel

### Genre Header

Each genre channel features a distinctive header that includes:

- **Genre name** — The name of the musical style
- **Visual background** — A cinematic background image representing the genre
- **Optional subtitle** — Additional context about the genre

### Content Sections

Genre channels organize content into multiple sections:

- **Top Beats** — Most popular beats in the genre
- **New Releases** — Recently uploaded content
- **Featured Albums** — Collections in this style
- **Top Producers** — Artists who excel in this genre
- **Related Playlists** — Curated compilations

The specific sections vary by genre and how much content is available.

### Custom Beat Requests

At the bottom of genre pages, you may see an option to submit a custom beat request. This allows you to request a beat specifically tailored to the genre you're browsing.

## Navigating Genre Channels

### Browsing Sections

Each section in a genre channel can be browsed like any other channel:
- Scroll horizontally to see more items
- Click **View more** to see the full section
- Click any item to view its details

### Starting Genre Radio

From a genre page, you can start a genre-based radio station:
1. Look for the options menu or **More** button
2. Select **Go to genre radio**
3. Enjoy a continuous mix of beats from that genre

### Exploring Related Content

Use genre channels as a jumping-off point:
- Click producers to explore their full catalogs
- Click beats to see similar content
- Discover producers you wouldn't have found otherwise

## Popular Genre Channels

While genres vary based on what producers upload, common genre channels include:

- **Hip-Hop** — Classic hip-hop beats and instrumentals
- **Trap** — Heavy 808s and modern trap productions
- **R&B** — Smooth, melodic beats
- **Pop** — Radio-friendly instrumentals
- **Drill** — Dark, sliding 808 patterns
- **Lo-Fi** — Chill, nostalgic beats
- **Afrobeats** — African-inspired rhythms

And many more depending on the community's uploads.

## Tips for Using Genre Channels

- **Start with broad genres** — If you're unsure what you want, browse major genres first
- **Explore sub-genres** — More specific genres give more targeted results
- **Check multiple sections** — Don't just look at top beats; explore new releases and albums too
- **Use genre radio** — Let BeatPass create a continuous mix while you work

<Tip>
Found a genre you love? Bookmark the genre channel page for quick access, or follow producers in that genre to see their new releases in your feed.
</Tip>
