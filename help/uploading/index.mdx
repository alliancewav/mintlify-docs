This section documents the current producer upload flow in BeatPass, including single-beat uploads, collections, metadata, cover art, sample-safe status, and audio fingerprinting.

---

## What the Current Upload Flow Supports

BeatPass producers can currently:

- Upload individual beats
- Use **Create Collection** to build a collection while uploading multiple beats
- Add metadata, cover art, and licensing settings before saving
- Replace audio later if needed

<Warning>
  The current producer upload flow only accepts **WAV** beat files. Even if older copy mentions other lossless formats, the live upload validation currently blocks non-WAV files.
</Warning>

---

## Upload Overview

When you upload a beat to BeatPass, the current flow looks like this:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the WAV file">
    Drag and drop or choose your beat file from the upload page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run duplicate check and fingerprinting">
    BeatPass checks the audio for duplicate content and prepares the fingerprint status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the track form">
    Add cover art, metadata, sample-safe status, and licensing settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the beat or collection">
    Once the required fields are complete, save the track or collection to publish it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Required Track Information

Before a new track can be saved, BeatPass currently requires:

- **Track Name**
- **Artists**
- **Cover Art**
- **Key**
- **Scale**
- **BPM**
- **Uploaded audio source**
- **Licensing model**

If you enable exclusive licensing, BeatPass also requires the exclusive contact email and exclusive status fields.

## After Uploading

Once a beat is saved:

- It appears on your producer profile
- It can participate in BeatPass licensing based on the model you selected
- You can return later to edit metadata, cover art, licensing, or audio
- Fingerprint and finances data continue to update over time
