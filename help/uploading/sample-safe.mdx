The **Sample-Safe(TM) Warranty** section tells BeatPass whether your beat is fully cleared or whether a buyer should expect extra sample-clearance work.

This is producer self-certification. BeatPass relies on the status you choose when showing sample-related warnings to buyers.

---

## Current Sample-Safe States

When uploading or editing a beat, BeatPass currently gives you two main choices:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Cleared" icon="shield">
    Use this when the beat is 100% original or all samples are fully cleared.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Needs Clearance" icon="warning">
    Use this when the beat includes samples that still require outside clearance.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Set It

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Protection & Verification">
    On the track form, go to the section that includes **Sample-Safe(TM) Warranty**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the status card">
    Click either **Cleared** or **Needs Clearance**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add notes if needed">
    BeatPass only shows the **Additional Notes** field when you choose **Needs Clearance**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the track">
    Your selected state is saved with the beat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Buyers See

The current UI behavior is:

- **Cleared** -> BeatPass shows the beat as sample-safe certified
- **Needs Clearance** -> BeatPass shows a buyer-facing clearance warning

That warning is important because it changes how comfortable a buyer may be using the beat commercially.

## What Counts as Cleared?

Choose **Cleared** only when one of these is true:

- The beat is built entirely from original material
- All sampled material is properly licensed or cleared
- The sample source is truly safe for your intended distribution

## What Counts as Needs Clearance?

Choose **Needs Clearance** when:

- You sampled another recording or composition without full clearance
- You used material that could trigger outside permissions
- You are unsure whether the sample use is fully cleared

<Warning>
  When in doubt, **Needs Clearance** is the safer choice. Mislabeling a beat as cleared can create legal and business problems for both you and the buyer.
</Warning>

## Notes for Uncleared Samples

If you choose **Needs Clearance**, use the notes field to add context such as:

- What type of sample was used
- Whether clearance is in progress
- Anything the buyer should understand before release

Keep the note short and practical.

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Document your sample sources">
    Keep receipts, licenses, and written permissions for sample packs and cleared content.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Update the status if things change">
    If you later clear the sample, come back and switch the beat from **Needs Clearance** to **Cleared**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do not treat this as legal advice">
    Sample clearance rules vary by situation. BeatPass helps surface the issue, but you are still responsible for your own sample use.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
