BeatPass's **AI Presets** are genre-matched cover art templates surfaced from the BeatPass artwork library. They are a fast way to add artwork without uploading your own image.

<Note>
  The current system matches prebuilt preset images from BeatPass's template library. Do not describe it as generating a brand-new bespoke image for every upload.
</Note>

---

## How AI Presets Work

BeatPass uses the genres on your track to look up matching preset artwork.

That means:

- Better genre choices usually produce better preset suggestions
- The preset list can change when your genres change
- Multiple producers can use the same preset

## Current Preset Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add genres to the track">
    Start by selecting the genres that best describe the beat.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the AI Presets area">
    BeatPass loads the matching presets in the cover-art section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a preset">
    Click the preset you want to use.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the track">
    The selected preset becomes the track's artwork once the beat is saved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Refreshing Presets

BeatPass currently includes a **Refresh** action in the preset area. Use it when:

- You changed the genres
- You want to see other matching options
- The current set does not fit the release

## If No Matching Presets Appear

If BeatPass does not show presets:

- Add or adjust the genres
- Try broader or more accurate genres
- Use [Custom Artwork](/help/uploading/cover-art/custom-art) instead

## Switching Between Presets and Custom Art

You can move between the two approaches:

- Selecting a preset replaces the current manual image choice
- Uploading a custom image clears the selected preset and switches the artwork mode back to manual

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pick genres before artwork">
    Genres are what drive the current preset matching logic.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use presets for speed, not forced uniqueness">
    Presets are great for fast, polished uploads, but they are not one-of-one artwork.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Switch to custom art if the release needs stronger branding">
    Presets are convenient, but your own visual system may still be better for premium or highly branded releases.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
