Your library is the personal side of BeatPass. It collects the music and tools you have already interacted with so you can get back to them quickly without browsing the full catalog again.

<Info>
  On desktop, opening **Library** takes you straight to **Your tracks**. On smaller screens, the top-level library page shows tiles for each library section.
</Info>

## Accessing Library Sections

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Desktop behavior">
    The left navigation includes **Library** and the individual library destinations. Opening **Library** routes to **Your tracks**, where you can switch into other sections such as **Collections**, **Producers**, **Play history**, **Beat requests**, **Licenses**, and **Lyrics**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Mobile behavior">
    The top-level library screen acts like a hub. It shows large shortcuts for **Beats**, **Playlists**, **Collections**, **Producers**, **Play history**, **Beat requests**, **Licenses**, and **Lyrics**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Account requirement">
    Library pages are tied to your account. You need to be signed in to save content and access these sections.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How Items Get Here

- Tracks and collections appear after you save them to your library.
- Producer profiles appear after you save that producer.
- Listening history is created automatically as you play music.
- Beat requests appear after you publish a request.
- Licenses appear when BeatPass generates a license record for your download or purchase.
- Lyrics appear after you write and save lyrics from the player lyrics editor.
