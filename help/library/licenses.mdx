The **Licenses** page is the central place to review BeatPass license records tied to your account.

## How to Get Here

Open **Your library** → **Licenses**. The page title is **Your licenses** with the subtitle **View and manage all your track licenses**.

## What You Can Do

- **Search** by track, artist, licensee, or license ID
- **Download** a printable certificate for any license you received
- **Verify License** to open the public verification page in a new tab
- Review licenses granted to other users if you own tracks on BeatPass

## What Appears on Each License Card

Each card shows:

- Track name (linked to the track page) and credited artists
- License type badge — non-exclusive (green) or exclusive (blue)
- Issued date
- Status badge (**Active**, **Revoked**, or **Warned**)
- License UUID

## Your Licenses vs Granted Licenses

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Your own licenses" icon="file-lines" defaultOpen>
    When you download a track as a non-owner, BeatPass automatically generates a license and it appears here.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Granted licenses" icon="users">
    If you are a track owner, this page also shows licenses issued to other users for your catalog. Granted licenses are identified by the licensee name.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Empty State

If you have no licenses yet, the page shows:

> **No licenses yet** — When you download tracks, licenses will automatically be generated and appear here for easy access.

## Important Notes

- The **Download** action on a license card opens the printable certificate page — it does not re-download the audio file.
- License records reflect the terms at the time of issuance. Each certificate includes BeatPassID fingerprinting data for authenticity verification.
- The library label in the sidebar is **Licenses** (not "My Licenses").
