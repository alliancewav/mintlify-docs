Downgrading lets you switch to a less expensive plan while keeping your current features until your next billing date. The new plan takes effect when your current period ends.

---

## How to Downgrade

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Billing Page">
    Click your **profile avatar** in the top navigation bar, then select **Billing** from the menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Change Plan">
    In the Current plan section, click the **Change plan** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Lower Plan">
    Choose a plan with a lower price than your current subscription.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the Change">
    BeatPass shows that the change will happen on your renewal date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Downgrade">
    Click **Confirm Plan Change**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## How Downgrades Work

Unlike upgrades, downgrades are **always scheduled** for your next renewal date:

| What Happens | When |
|--------------|------|
| You confirm the downgrade | Now |
| You keep current plan features | Until your next renewal |
| New plan activates | At your next renewal date |
| You're charged the new, lower price | Starting from renewal |

<Note>
  BeatPass keeps your current plan until the next billing date. The downgrade does not happen immediately.
</Note>

---

## Pending Downgrade

After scheduling a downgrade, Billing shows a **Scheduled Plan Change** card with:

- **Current plan** — What you have now
- **Pending plan** — What you're switching to
- **Effective date** — When the change happens

### Cancelling a Pending Downgrade

Changed your mind? You can cancel a scheduled downgrade before it takes effect:

1. Click your **profile avatar** → **Billing**
2. Find the **Scheduled Plan Change** card showing your pending downgrade
3. Click **Cancel scheduled change**
4. Your subscription continues as normal

---

## What Changes On Renewal

The most common change is the monthly beat request limit:

| Plan | Beat Request Limit |
|------|--------------------|
| **Classic** | 1 |
| **Plus** | 3 |
| **Pro** | 5 |

<Warning>
  Review the destination plan before confirming. When the downgrade takes effect, the new plan's limits and included features apply.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I downgrade immediately?">
    Downgrades are always scheduled for your next renewal to ensure you receive the full value of your current billing period. You've already paid for your current plan until that date.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I undo a scheduled downgrade?">
    Yes. Use **Cancel scheduled change** on the **Scheduled Plan Change** card before the renewal date.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
