## Welcome, Artists

BeatPass gives you access to thousands of quality beats from independent producers, with transparent licensing and fair pricing.

<Info>
  **Time to complete:** 10-15 minutes for basic setup
</Info>

---

## Prerequisites

Before you begin:

- Active BeatPass account with verified email
- Understanding of how you'll use licensed beats (commercial vs personal)
- Payment method ready for licensing purchases

---

## Step-by-Step Onboarding

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete Your Artist Profile">
    A strong profile helps producers understand your style:
    - Upload a profile photo
    - Write a bio describing your music and goals
    - Link your social profiles or portfolio
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore the Beat Catalog">
    Learn to navigate the platform:
    - **Browse** - Explore by genre, mood, or trending
    - **Filter** - Narrow by BPM, key, duration, and tags
    - **Search** - Find specific producers or track names
    - **Preview** - Stream full tracks before committing
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Build Your Library">
    Organize beats for your projects:
    - Click the heart icon to save beats
    - Access saved beats in your Library
    - Create playlists to group beats by project or vibe
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Understand Licensing Options">
    Before purchasing, know your options:
    
    | License Type | What You Get | Best For |
    |--------------|--------------|----------|
    | Non-Exclusive | Use rights, beat stays available | Mixtapes, demos, limited distribution |
    | Exclusive | Full rights, beat removed from sale | Commercial releases, major projects |
    
    Each beat displays its available licensing options and pricing.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Your First Purchase">
    When you're ready to license:
    1. Click "License" or "Buy" on the track page
    2. Select your license tier
    3. Complete secure checkout
    4. Download high-quality files and license certificate
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect with Producers (Optional)">
    Build relationships for custom work:
    - Follow producers whose style you love
    - Message them about custom beats or collaborations
    - Post beat requests describing your needs
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Finding the Right Beats

### Search Strategies

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="By Project Needs">
    Know your tempo and mood before searching. Filter by BPM range and select relevant mood tags.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="By Producer">
    Found a producer you like? Follow them and explore their full catalog.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="By Trend">
    Check "Trending" for beats gaining popularity - these are crowd-validated.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="By Beat Request">
    Post what you need publicly. Let producers come to you with options.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Filtering Tips

- **BPM**: Match to your rap style (80-90 for slow, 140+ for drill)
- **Key**: Consider your vocal range
- **Duration**: Ensure it fits your song structure
- **Tags**: Use mood tags like "dark", "melodic", "hard"

## Licensing Best Practices

<Warning>
  Always verify the license type before using a beat. Using a beat beyond your license terms is a violation.
</Warning>

### Before You Buy

1. Preview the full track
2. Check license terms and pricing
3. Verify the beat fits your project needs
4. Download and save your license certificate

### After Purchase

- Store license certificates safely
- Credit producers as required by license terms
- Track which beats are used in which projects
- Keep receipts for tax purposes

## Beat Requests

Can't find what you need? Post a request:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe Your Needs">
    Be specific about genre, tempo, mood, and references.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Budget">
    Indicate what you're willing to pay (optional but helpful).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Responses">
    Producers will send you options that match your description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Negotiate & Purchase">
    Work directly with producers on pricing and terms.
  </Step>
</Steps>
