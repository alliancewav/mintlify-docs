## Overview

Track insights (`/backstage/tracks/{id}/insights`) provide the most detailed analytics available on BeatPass. They include engagement metrics unique to individual tracks, helping you understand exactly how listeners interact with your music.

<Info>
  **Access:** Right-click any track ‚Üí Insights, or navigate directly to `/backstage/tracks/{trackId}/insights`
</Info>

---

## Available Charts

Track insights display the following analytics:

| Chart | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Engagement Overview** | Quick summary of listener interaction metrics |
| **Plays Over Time** | Interactive bar chart with trend indicator |
| **Listen Duration Chart** | How long listeners engage over time |
| **Completion Breakdown** | Distribution of how much listeners complete |
| **Top Devices** | Device types (desktop, mobile, tablet) |
| **Top Platforms** | Operating systems and platforms |
| **Top Locations** | Geographic distribution with country flags |
| **Top Listeners** | Your biggest fans for this track |

## Plays Over Time

An interactive bar chart displaying play counts over your selected date range.

### Chart Elements

- **Header:** Title, trend badge (growth/decline %), total plays
- **Bars:** Each bar represents plays for a time period (day, week, or month)
- **X-Axis:** Date labels shown intelligently based on data density
- **Footer:** Peak Day, Daily Avg, Data Points

### Trend Indicator

| Badge Color | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Green (+)** | Plays increased | +36% more plays than before |
| **Red (-)** | Plays decreased | -71% fewer plays than before |
| **Orange (0%)** | No significant change | Stable performance |

### Reading Patterns

| Pattern | What It Means | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| **Spike** | Viral moment or playlist placement | Identify cause and replicate |
| **Steady bars** | Consistent organic engagement | Keep doing what works |
| **Gradual increase** | Growing audience | Continue current strategy |
| **Gradual decline** | Waning interest | Consider promotion |
| **Sharp drop** | Sudden traffic loss | Investigate playlist removal |

## Listen Duration Chart

An interactive bar chart showing how long listeners engage with your track over time.

### Chart Elements

- **Header:** Clock icon, trend badge, average duration
- **Bars:** Each bar represents average listen duration for that day
- **Bar Colors:** Color-coded by completion rate (green/amber/red)
- **Footer:** Track length, best day, avg completion, total plays

### Bar Colors

| Color | Completion Rate | Meaning |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| **Green** | ‚â•70% completed | Excellent ‚Äî listeners hear most of the track |
| **Amber/Yellow** | 40-70% completed | Good ‚Äî engaged but drop off before end |
| **Red** | < 40% completed | Needs attention ‚Äî listeners skip early |

<Tip>
  If you see mostly red bars, consider reviewing your track's intro. The first 15-30 seconds are critical for retention.
</Tip>

## Completion Breakdown Chart

A grid showing how much of your track listeners complete, broken into five categories.

### Categories

| Category | Icon | Range | What It Means |
|----------|------|-------|---------------|
| **Completed** | ‚úì (green) | >80% | Heard almost all of your track |
| **Mostly** | ‚óâ (blue) | 60-80% | Strong engagement, dropped near end |
| **Half** | ‚óã (amber) | 40-60% | Heard about half the track |
| **Partial** | ‚äò (orange) | 20-40% | Limited listen, lost interest |
| **Skipped** | ‚è© (red) | < 20% | Skipped very early |

### Quality Score

A single number (0-100) summarizing overall listener retention:

| Score | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **80-100** | Excellent | Listeners love your track |
| **60-79** | Good | Solid engagement with room to improve |
| **40-59** | Fair | Many listeners drop off |
| **20-39** | Needs Work | Most listeners leave early |
| **0-19** | Poor | Almost everyone skips quickly |

<Warning>
  A high "Skipped" count often indicates issues with your intro. Consider starting with a stronger hook.
</Warning>
